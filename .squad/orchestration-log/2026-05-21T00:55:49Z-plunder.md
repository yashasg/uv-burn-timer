## Verdict: ⚠️ BORDERLINE → REWRITE REQUIRED

**Short answer:** Apple is NOT a valid analogue. They display an atmospheric scalar (temperature, cloud cover). We personalize an output (UVI × Fitzpatrick × SPF → burn time). The personalization triggers health-adjacent compliance obligations.

---

## Legal Anchors

| Regulation | Citation | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| MDCG 2019-11 | §3.3 | Medical device guidance; personalized health outputs require risk disclosure |
| FDA General Wellness (2019) | §III.B | Wellness claims must include risk disclaimers if output affects user behavior |
| FDA 2019 | §III.B.2 | Foreseeable-misuse doctrine: if photosensitized users will use the forecast, disclose |
| FTC § 5 | Deceptive omission | Omitting material risk (photosensitivity) from a product that outputs health-adjacent claims is unfair/deceptive |
| MDR Annex VIII Rule 11 | EU MDR | Labeling / instructions requirement for products affecting health decisions |
| UK MHRA 2022 | General guidance | Medical device boundary: personalized health output crosses into regulatory scope if foreseeable harm is material |

## Factual Risk Assessment

**Photosensitized users (D-2026-05-19-007):** A cohort taking photosensitizing drugs (NSAID, fluoroquinolone, thiazide, etc.) faces higher UVI sensitivity than Fitzpatrick alone predicts. This cohort is **foreseeable** — we enumerate them in L4 (About sheet). **Silence on the forecast surface** while they use the picker is non-compliant regardless of Apple's posture.

**Why Apple is not a valid analogue:**
- Apple: displays atmospheric `UVIndex` (meteorological scalar, no personalization).
- Us: displays `BurnTime` (personalized health output via `UVI × Fitzpatrick × SPF → t_burn`).
- The personalization crosses the regulatory threshold. Apple's silence does not transfer.
